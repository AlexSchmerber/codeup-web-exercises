Must Visit National Parks

Yosemite
Yosemite stands out for its multitude of amazing waterfalls and also includes many hiking trails and campsites for park goers.
Yellowstone
The worlds first national park, Yellowstone makes a name for itself with its 2.2 million acres of unique natural attractions and biodiversity.
Glacier National Park
Most known for its scenic water filled valleys that lay under the beautiful mountain-scape, this National Park includes 2 mountain ranges and over 700 lakes.
Grand Canyon
Known for its grandiose view, this National Parks beauty must be viewed in person to truly understand its magnificent scope.
Zion National Park
One of the best National Parks for thrill-seekers, Zions attractions include a 1,500-foot staircase up a mountain path, suitably named "Angels Landing".
Grand Teton
Offering 200 miles of trails that wind down prairies, lakes, and the Parks most notable sight, the Grand Teton snow-capped mountain range.
Bryce Canyon
Another Canyon thats wonders were shaped by the ever changing natural forces of erosion, like the Grand Canyon, Bryce Canyon is adorned with Hoodoos which are these wondrous irregularly shaped rock structures.
Rocky Mountains
One of the most convenient National Parks, the Rockies stand roughly 70 miles from Denver making them one of the most accessible natural beauty to frequent.
Arches
77,000 acres of Utah's oddly shaped sandstone formations that provide unique views to hikers and climbers.
Smoky Mountains
This mountain range provides plenty of opportunities to view North Carolina and Tennessee's beautiful landscapes and wildlife.

Arches

  • In 1929 President Herbert Hoover created Arches National Monument, then in 1971 Congress designated Arches as a National Park under the Nixon administration.
  • There are more than 2000 natural sandstone arches inside Arches National Park.
  • Landscape Arch has the longest span of any arch in North America, 306 feet (93.3 meters), and is the fourth longest in the world
  • The enormous sandstone boulder atop Balanced Rock is presumed to weigh approximately 3,577 tons!

Badlands

  • Bison, commonly known as buffalo, can be seen throughout the park.
  • Badlands lies in an ecological area known as a mixed-grass prairie.
  • The Badlands National Park area is known worldwide for its large number of fossil remains.
  • The name Badlands comes from the Lakota, who first called it "mako sica", or "land that is bad".

Joshua Tree

  • As part of the Desert Protection Bill, Joshua Tree National Monument was elevated to national park status on October 31, 1994.
  • Early explorer John Fremont described Joshua trees as "the most repulsive tree in the vegetable Kingdom".
  • Mormon pioneers thought the limbs of the Joshua trees resembled the upstretched arms of Joshua leading them to the promised land.
  • More than 550,000 acres of Joshua Tree National Park's nearly 800,000 acres are designated as wilderness.
